Why would I run this with an outside operator instead of in-house?

Because the team you have is already running the business. Wolf Pack is fulfilling and selling, Quantum Cipher Mentorship is converting at 124 members, the Quantum Cipher Report ships every Monday, YouTube and X are publishing. Adding a 90-day VSL build, a dedicated lead-magnet page, a cohort tier, and a paid acquisition layer on top of an already-loaded team is how those projects either get postponed by a year or get launched at half the quality. Outside operator is faster, cleaner, and exits when production hits steady state. We hand the system to your team after the launch cycle, not before.

How much of my time does this need?

One day to film the iknkfx.com VSL. One day to film the Funded Trader Operator Cohort positioning. Calendar holds for cohort delivery in Lever 02. The rest is build team work plus you reviewing the copy, creative, and pricing decisions before they ship. Most operators at your stage spend under 8 hours a month during the 90-day build, and under 2 hours a month after the system is in production.

Will the Cohort cannibalise Quantum Cipher Mentorship?

No. The Cohort and Quantum Cipher Mentorship solve different problems. Mentorship is structured curriculum plus alerts plus group access for traders working through the methodology. The Cohort is strategic operating-room access for traders who already cleared the methodology, traded six months consistently profitable, and are ready to scale account size and risk discipline at portfolio level. Different deliverable, different audience, different buying motion. Most Cohort applicants will be Mentorship graduates moving up the ladder, not net-new buyers choosing Cohort over Mentorship. Where there's overlap, the Cohort is the explicit "post-Mentorship" tier. It expands the ladder, it doesn't compete inside it.

How does this work alongside the existing Wolf Pack alerts?

Wolf Pack is the $997 course-plus-alerts product on Whop. The Cohort sits two tiers above it on the ladder, capturing the operator who's already trading Quantum Cipher live, has cleared the Mentorship, and wants peer-room access at portfolio scale. Different format, different price, different deliverable. Wolf Pack alumni who graduate into Mentorship and then into the Cohort are the cleanest ascension path. Where applicable, Cohort graduates can elect into ongoing Mentorship-tier access afterwards, completing the ladder.

What if my existing Mentorship already covers some of what the Cohort would?

There's overlap on some content (setup quality, position sizing, risk math), but the deliverable is different. Mentorship is "we teach you the methodology and group-coach you through it." Cohort is "you've already proven six months consistently profitable, now we operate at peer-room scale, audit your live account, and scale risk discipline beyond a small account." Most Cohort attendees will have already passed through Mentorship and outgrown the curriculum. The overlap is feature, not bug. Cohort buys insider continuity for operators who don't want to start from scratch with another educator.
